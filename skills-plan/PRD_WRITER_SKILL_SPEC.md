# prd-writer Skill Spec

## Skill Name

prd-writer

## Purpose

Turn ambiguous product ideas into clear, testable product requirements.

## Trigger Scenarios

- The user asks for a PRD, feature requirement, product spec, or requirement clarification.
- The product idea is promising but lacks clear users, scenarios, scope, or acceptance criteria.
- A requirement needs to be prepared for design and engineering handoff.

## Inputs

- Product idea
- Target user
- Scenario and pain point
- Business goal
- Constraints
- Existing context or references

## Workflow

1. Clarify user, scenario, pain point, goal, value, MVP, and risk.
2. Separate assumptions from confirmed facts.
3. Define functional and non-functional requirements.
4. Add acceptance criteria and open questions.
5. Keep scope focused on the next buildable version.

## Outputs

- PRD structure
- User stories
- Requirement list
- Acceptance criteria
- Risks and open questions

## Quality Checklist

- User and scenario are explicit.
- MVP scope is clear.
- Requirements are testable.
- Risks and assumptions are visible.
- Engineering handoff is actionable.

## Guardrails

- Do not over-design.
- Do not invent business facts.
- Do not skip AI necessity evaluation when AI features are involved.
