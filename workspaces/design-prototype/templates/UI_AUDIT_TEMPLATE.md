## 3. Overall Impression

Summarize the first impression and whether the design matches the product goal.

## 4. Information Architecture

Evaluate whether content grouping, navigation, priority, and user path are clear.

## 5. Visual Hierarchy

Evaluate whether the eye is guided from primary content to primary action.

## 6. Layout and Spacing

Evaluate grid, alignment, density, whitespace, rhythm, and responsive behavior.

## 7. Typography

Evaluate font hierarchy, readability, weight, size, line height, and consistency.

## 8. Color

Evaluate palette, contrast, semantic color, brand fit, and action emphasis.

## 9. Components

Evaluate buttons, cards, forms, tabs, navigation, icons, modals, and reusable components.

## 10. Interaction States

Evaluate default, hover, pressed, focused, disabled, selected, success, warning, and error states.

## 11. Empty / Loading / Error States

Evaluate whether non-happy paths are helpful, calm, and actionable.

## 12. Accessibility

Evaluate contrast, keyboard/focus behavior, touch target size, readable labels, and motion sensitivity.

## 13. Mobile Adaptation

Evaluate small-screen layout, touch ergonomics, bottom actions, safe areas, and content density.
