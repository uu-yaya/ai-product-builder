# YouTube AI Research Workflow

## 1. Purpose

Extract useful AI product signals from YouTube while avoiding hype and unverified claims.

## 2. When to Use

Use when researching creator commentary, demos, tutorials, product walkthroughs, or community reaction videos.

## 3. Inputs

- Topic
- Video list or search query
- Timeframe
- Channel preferences
- Product question

## 4. Steps

1. Select videos by relevance, recency, channel credibility, evidence quality, and information density.
2. Evaluate channel expertise, incentives, sponsorship, and track record.
3. Extract key claims.
4. Cross-validate claims with official sources, docs, papers, GitHub, product pages, or trusted media.
5. Identify titlebait, hype, unsupported claims, and opinions.
6. Convert useful signals into PM insight and demo ideas.
7. List what needs further verification.

## 5. Outputs

- Video list
- Channel credibility assessment
- Key claims
- Evidence check
- PM insight
- Technical insight
- Demo ideas
- Hype signals
- Verification needs

## 6. Quality Checklist

- Channel credibility is assessed.
- Claims are separated from evidence.
- Hype is called out.
- Claims are cross-validated before treated as facts.
- Demo ideas are tied to product value.

## 7. Common Mistakes

- Trusting a video because it is popular.
- Treating opinions as facts.
- Ignoring sponsorship incentives.
- Copying the creator's conclusion without verification.
- Missing practical product implications.
