# Paper to Product Insight Workflow

## 1. Purpose

Turn papers or technical blogs into product insights, feature ideas, demo ideas, learning notes, and engineering considerations.

## 2. When to Use

Use when reading arXiv papers, research blogs, technical deep dives, benchmarks, or model/system papers.

## 3. Inputs

- Paper or blog link
- Research question
- Product domain
- Desired depth
- Known technical constraints

## 4. Steps

1. Quickly identify problem, core idea, method, result, and limitations.
2. Separate research claims from product assumptions.
3. Judge whether the idea is productizable.
4. Translate findings into possible features and use cases.
5. Identify required data, model, infrastructure, evaluation, and UX needs.
6. Output learning notes and demo idea.

## 5. Outputs

- Paper summary
- Problem / method / result / limitations
- Product interpretation
- Possible use cases
- Demo idea
- Required data / tech
- Risks
- Learning notes

## 6. Quality Checklist

- Limitations are included.
- Productization gap is explicit.
- Required data and engineering work are visible.
- Demo idea is small enough to test.
- Research result is not overstated.

## 7. Common Mistakes

- Treating benchmark gains as product readiness.
- Ignoring data requirements.
- Ignoring UX and workflow fit.
- Missing limitations.
- Producing feature ideas without target users.
